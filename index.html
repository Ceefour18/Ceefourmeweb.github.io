About Me

Clinical Genomics & Cancer Bioinformatics

From raw sequencing data to clinically interpretable variant calls.

Clinical bioinformatician specializing in cancer genomics — building reproducible WES and WGS somatic variant calling pipelines with GATK4/Mutect2. My work targets clinically relevant breast cancer genes — TP53, BRCA1, ERBB2 — from raw FASTQ through annotated, filtered VCF.

Grounded in RNA-seq differential expression analysis, I am now diving into multi-omics integration — bulk RNA-seq, scRNA-seq cell-type deconvolution, and somatic variant landscapes — to build a unified view of breast cancer biology for precision oncology roles.

Flagship · WES · GATK4

Somatic WES Breast Cancer Pipeline (GATK4)

182 → 27 PASS variants TP53 VAF 98.6% Contam. 1.11% 99.47% mapped

Docker-based GATK4 tumour-normal WES pipeline on HCC1143/HCC1143BL, targeting chr17 (TP53, BRCA1, ERBB2). FilterMutectCalls reduced raw calls by 85% (182 → 27 PASS); recovered clonal TP53 p.Arg248Gln driver at 98.6% VAF; C>T transitions at 52% consistent with SBS1/SBS5 ageing signatures.

Fully version-controlled — Dockerfile, reference genome hashes, and parameter logs committed to repo.
